Use AI to Strengthen Your CV and Job Search

AI training for job seekers shows candidates how to use tools such as ChatGPT, Claude and Gemini to support employer research, CV tailoring and interview preparation. The programme teaches responsible use. AI can help organise and improve an application, but it should never invent qualifications, experience, responsibilities or results.

Who this training is for

  • People returning to employment
  • Graduates and early-career applicants
  • Career changers
  • Professionals seeking promotion
  • Applicants unfamiliar with current AI tools

No coding or previous AI experience is required.

Three practical benefits

  • Tailor applications more effectively. Learn how to identify relevant requirements in a job description and connect them to your real skills and experience
  • Present genuine achievements clearly. Use AI to improve structure and wording without inventing experience or making unsupported claims
  • Prepare for interviews with confidence. Generate role-relevant practice questions and use structured exercises to develop clearer, evidence-based answers

What the training covers

  • Analysing job descriptions
  • Researching employers and sectors
  • Improving CV structure and clarity
  • Drafting cover-letter frameworks
  • Preparing competency-based examples
  • Practising interview questions
  • Reviewing AI output for exaggeration or invented details
  • Protecting personal information when using AI services

Responsible use

AI should help candidates communicate real experience more clearly. It should not create false qualifications, fictional employment history or achievements that cannot be explained during an interview. Before submitting an application, candidates should review every sentence and ensure it is accurate, personal and relevant. The programme does not guarantee interviews, employment or a particular salary.

Frequently asked questions

Can AI write my CV for me?

AI can help organise and improve your existing information, but you must verify every statement and make the final document genuinely yours.

Can employers detect AI-written applications?

Detection tools are unreliable, but generic or impersonal writing can weaken an application. The better approach is to use AI as an editing and preparation assistant.

Should I paste my existing CV into a public AI tool?

Consider what personal information the document contains and review the platform's privacy settings first. Remove unnecessary contact details and sensitive information where appropriate.

Does the training guarantee that I will get a job?

No. Recruitment decisions depend on the role, employer, competition and the candidate's experience. The training is designed to help applicants prepare and communicate more effectively.
